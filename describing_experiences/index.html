Describing Experiences

Functional English

Level: Low Int ESL 404/412

Let's Learn

How was your holiday? What did you do? What was the best part? In this lesson, you will practice describing past experiences, including good ones, bad ones, and everything in between.

Warm-Up: Brainstorming

Think of some experiences that people often describe. Work with your classmates and list them below. (Examples: vacations, sporting events, job interviews)

Made with

Common Questions

Listen/read and repeat the following common questions.

  • How was your weekend / holiday / trip?
  • Where did you go?
  • What did you do?
  • How did it go?
  • Why? What happened?
  • Did you do anything fun / exciting / interesting?

Dialogues

Listen and follow along. Practice describing different types of experiences.

Dialogue 1: A Great Experience

👩
A: Welcome back! How was your winter break?
👨
B: It was great, thanks.
👩
A: Did you do anything fun?
👨
B: Yeah. We went to New York City!
👩
A: Nice! How was it?
👨
B: It was awesome. We ate great food and saw amazing shows. We also went skating at Rockefeller Center.
👩
A: Sounds exciting!

Dialogue 2: A Disappointing Experience

👱‍♀️
A: Hey! How was that new restaurant?
👱‍♂️
B: It was terrible.
👱‍♀️
A: Oh really? What was wrong with it?
👱‍♂️
B: Well, first the service was horrible. Then the food was cold. It was really expensive too.
👱‍♀️
A: That's too bad.
👱‍♂️
B: Yeah. We were really disappointed.
👱‍♀️
A: No doubt!

Dialogue 3: An Interesting Experience

👧
A: Did you get that job?
👦
B: Yes! I started on Monday.
👧
A: How was your first day?
👦
B: Well, it was [pauses] interesting.
👧
A: Why? What happened?
👦
B: Well, first they showed me how to work the till. Then they left me all alone in the store!
👧
A: Really? How did that go?
👦
B: It went well, actually. I worked till 10:00 pm and didn't make any major mistakes.
👧
A: Good for you!
